Tips and tricks

Thin and sheer fabrics

If you use very tin fabrics like silk, they tend to slip away when you're cutting it. Then place a table cloth underneath it. That way it won't slip.

When you have tin or sheer fabrics it is better to slice it than to cut it. That way it won't fray as much.

Synthetics

For cutting synthetic fabrics like lining you can use pinking shears

Stretch fabrics

Use a stretch stitch so the fabric stays stretchy.

Whle sewing this fabric don't pull it too much.

For all fabrics

If you have to use a seam ripper use it with the little bal down. That way it opens up the seam, and it is easier to rip the seam.

Take a margin for 1 to 2 centimeters for the length of your tread. In case you need to take it out.

Place an elastic over the side of you sewing machine, besides the needle. If you let your fabric run past it, you'll always sew straight.

Choose the right needle by the right fabric. For every fabric is a needle.
